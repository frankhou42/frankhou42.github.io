I'm an undergraduate in Computer Science at the **University of Texas at Austin** (B.S., expected
May 2027), working at the intersection of **machine learning** and **systems**. I'm a research
assistant in UT's Machine Intelligence through Decision-making lab, where I study **offline
reinforcement learning** — benchmarking learned value functions and diagnosing failure modes in
model-based planning.

I've interned at **Amazon** (Shipping), building low-latency data infrastructure — moving schedule
serving from live DynamoDB reads to versioned S3 snapshots — and at **Scale AI** (GenAI / AI-safety
evaluation), designing rubrics and deterministic test suites for large-scale model evaluation.

I like problems where careful engineering and model behavior meet: **LLM evaluation**, **on-device
inference**, and the distributed systems that make models fast and reliable in production. Some of
that shows up in my [projects](/projects/) — a native macOS client for local LLMs, an autograd
engine built from scratch, and more.

Find my work on [GitHub](https://github.com/frankhou42), connect on
[LinkedIn](https://linkedin.com/in/yufanhou), or grab my [CV](/assets/pdf/cv.pdf).
